Our Core Message

The housing crisis has been affecting cities across the country especially major ones like Los Angeles. According to recent reports, 57 percent of Los Angeles residents are considered rent burdened meaning that as much as 47 percent of their income is going to rent. On top of that, about 53,000 people are homeless. These numbers have only been increasing throughout the years as population and demand increase. The reason why there is such an imbalance is due to the supply and demand offset. It is estimated that Los Angeles will need to create about 1.8 million to 3.5 million to meet minimum demands and as of now, the construction pace is falling short of about 100,000 houses per year.

Trying to fix this imbalance is a hard problem, but our group believes that we can aleve this pressure by helping home buyers get the best deal. Based on the Los Angeles County housing open dataset, we will test different model to accurately predict housing prices. Once we are confident with a model, we will create a user interface (website) to give the user an easy way to find the best house for his or her price point and region. We hope that this product can give home buyers more tools to making the best decision and not be rent burdened.

We are here to help.

This project will be an important tool that can be used for current or future renters or homeowners to effectively estimate the future cost of prospective homes and therefore provide insight into the optimal house given their desired region and lifestyle. We hope that when people use this tool, they will avoid making large financial mistakes which could cost them dearly. By helping the renter, this will also help the homeowners indirectly. Our tool will direct renters to optimal housing, meaning they can provide a steady stream of affordable income because they will have anticipated (via our tool) any price increases. If our tool becomes widespread, this will help optimize the housing market and actually provide people with reliable housing information. We hope that by doing this, we can minimize homelessness and costly housing situations so people will have more of their income to spend on resources other than housing.
